What is Web 1.0

Web 1.0 refers to the early stage of the World Wide Web, which was characterized by static web pages and limited interactivity. During this era, websites were primarily informational and lacked dynamic content or user-generated contributions. Users could only consume content created by webmasters, and there was little to no interaction between users and websites. Web 1.0 was focused on delivering information rather than facilitating communication or collaboration.
